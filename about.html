ABOUT Poet

Developed by The DIAGRAM Center (Digital Image And Graphic Resources for Accessible Materials), a Benetech Global Literacy initiative, Poet is an web based image description training tool that helps people learn when and how to describe various types of images frequently found in educational books. Poet users have access to reference guides and hands-on practice exercises that help cultivate skills for writing effective image descriptions for critical visual content.

The content found in this Poet training site was made possible through key partnerships with the Carl and Ruth Shapiro Family National Center for Accessible Media at WGBH (NCAM) and Touch Graphics, Inc. The current version of our web-based resource is broken up into three key sections. The first section guides users through the process of determining when a description is needed. The second section provides a general guidelines on how to produce an effective description for any type of image. The last section presents guidelines, examples, and the opportunity to practice writing descriptions for specific types of images (e.g., photos versus maps versus charts).

We are excited to share this resource with all of you and would like to acknowledge the key role that the DIAGRAM community and Poet supporters have provided throughout the evolution of this project. Special thanks to the dozens of “image slam” and “describathon” volunteers who provided invaluable feedback, and without whom, this resource would not have been possible. We hope this reference will be helpful to all of you and encourage you to share it with others still learning about image accessibility. And as with all DIAGRAM Center products, please use our brief online survey or contact us at info@diagramcenter.org to share any feedback and suggestions you might have about our Poet training tool. Thank you for your support!
